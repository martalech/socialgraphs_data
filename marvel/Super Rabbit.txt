Cover artist unknown\n|character_name=Super Rabbit\n|real_name=Waffles Bunny\n|publisher=[[Timely Comics]]\n|debut=''Comedy Comics'' #14 (Mar. 1943)\n|creators=[[Ernie Hart]]\n|alliances=[[Lockjaw and the Pet Avengers|Pet Avengers]]\n|aliases= The Marvel of the Age\n|powers=Super strength<br/>Flight<br/>Invulnerability except for a single spot at top of head\n}}\n\n'''Super Rabbit''' is a [[fictional character|fictional]], [[talking animals in fiction|talking animal]] [[superhero]] in [[comic books]] published by [[Timely Comics]], a predecessor of [[Marvel Comics]], during the 1930s and 1940s period fans and historians call the [[Golden Age of comic books]]. Created by [[cartoonist]] [[Ernie Hart]], he [[first appeared]] in ''Comedy Comics'' #14 ([[cover-date]]d Mar. 1943).<ref>{{cite book |last1=Mitchell |first1=Kurt |last2=Thomas |first2=Roy |title=American Comic Book Chronicles: 1940-1944 |date=2019 |publisher=TwoMorrows Publishing |isbn=978-1605490892 |page=192}}</ref>\n\nThe character appeared after [[Fawcett Comics]]' talking-animal superhero [[Hoppy the Marvel Bunny]] (debut: ''Fawcett's Funny Animals'' #1, cover-dated Dec. 1942), and before the [[Bugs Bunny]] theatrical [[cartoon]] short \"[[Super-Rabbit]]\" (released in April 1943).\n\n==Publication history==\nFollowing his debut as the cover star of ''Comedy Comics'' #14 (March 1943), Super Rabbit remained the lead feature through #33 (Sept. 1946). A star of Timely Comics' [[humor]] division&nbsp;\u2014 produced by what the company called its \"animator bullpen\", edited by [[Vincent Fago]] and largely separate from the [[superhero]] group producing comics featuring [[Captain America]] and other such characters<ref>{{cite web|last=Vassallo |first= Michael J. | url=http://www.comicartville.com/vincefago.htm | title=Vincent Fago and the Timely Funny Animal Dept. | publisher= Comicartville.com | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20081208161111/http://www.comicartville.com/vincefago.htm |url-status=dead | archive-date= December 8, 2008}}</ref>&nbsp;\u2014 Super Rabbit also appeared in ''Krazy Comics'', ''Comic Capers'', ''Funny Tunes'' (a.k.a. ''Animated Funny Comic-Tunes''), ''All Surprise Comics'' (as the cover star of #1-11, Fall 1943 - Fall 1946) and other [[anthology]] series.<ref name=gcd>[http://www.comics.org/character/name/super%20rabbit/sort/alpha/ Super Rabbit (character)] at the [[Grand Comics Database]]</ref>\n\nHe additionally starred in his own ''Super Rabbit Comics'', which ran 14 issues (Fall 1944 - Nov. 1948). His final story appeared in ''It's a Duck's Life'' #11 (Feb. 1952).<ref name=gcd />\n\nWhile a series of authorized reprints of Super Rabbit's adventures was published in Canada by [[Bell Features]],<ref>Bell, John (2006). A third issue, labeled #7 and costing 10\u00a2, later appeared, and was reissued in 1963 as #10 and costing 12\u00a2.<ref name=gcd />\n\nAside from creator Ernie Hart, other artists who contributed to his adventures included [[Mike Sekowsky]], [[Al Jaffee]], and inker [[Violet Barclay]].<ref name=gcd />\n\nIn 1977, Marvel announced a reboot of the character in a solo series with Marv Wolfman as writer, but apparently this project did not come to fruition.<ref>Letters page of ''[[Marvel Two-In-One]]'' #30, August 1977</ref>\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nMeek little Waffles Bunny, variously depicted as a reporter or a [[shoeshine boy]], rubs a [[magic ring]] to gain mass and height and become the flying, super-strong Super Rabbit.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Becattini |first1=Alberto |title=American Funny Animal Comics in the 20th Century: Volume Two |date=2019 |publisher=Theme Park Press |isbn=978-1683902218 |chapter=Super-Animals}}</ref> He is virtually invulnerable except for a small spot on the very top of his head, where something as light as a falling feather could knock him out. He protects the innocent, captures robbers, and even fights such [[World War II]] menaces as Super Nazi, a pig with a [[Hitler]] mustache.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Nevins |first1=Jess |title=Encyclopedia of Golden Age Superheroes |date=2013 |publisher=High Rock Press |isbn=978-1-61318-023-5 |page=262}}</ref> His self-proclaimed \"number-one fan\" and unasked-for publicist Wilbur Woodpecker occasionally accompanies Super Rabbit, much to his consternation.<ref>[http://www.toonopedia.com/superrab.htm Super Rabbit] at [[Don Markstein's Toonopedia]]. [https://www.webcitation.org/62voLIn1W?url=http://www.toonopedia.com/superrab.htm Archived] from the original on November 3, 2011.</ref>\n\nGenerally tall and lanky, the blue-clad, red-caped and orange-gloved and booted \"Marvel of the Age\" sometimes takes on a more buff and bulky form. His original costume featured the initial \"S\" on his chest, which he later replaced with the words \"SUPER RABBIT\".\n\n==In other media==\nA rabbit named Waffles appeared in the animated series ''[[M.O.D.O.K.
