Created by [[Roy Thomas]] and [[Sal Buscema]], the character made his first appearance in ''[[The Avengers (comic book)|The Avengers]]'' #69 (Oct. 1969) as a member of the [[Squadron Sinister]] known as the [[Whizzer (comics)|Whizzer]].<ref>{{cite book |last =Rovin |first =Jeff |title =The Encyclopedia of Supervillains |publisher =Facts on File |date =1987 |location =New York |isbn = 0-8160-1356-X |page=324}}</ref>\n\n==Publication history==\nJames Sanders first appears as the '''Whizzer''' in the final panel of ''[[The Avengers (comic book)|The Avengers]]'' #69 (Oct. 1969), the first chapter of a three-issue storyline by writer [[Roy Thomas]] and penciller [[Sal Buscema]]. The story arc introduced the [[supervillain]] team the [[Squadron Sinister]], whose four members were loosely based on heroes in [[DC Comics]]' [[Justice League|Justice League of America]], with the Whizzer based on the [[Flash (Barry Allen)|Flash]].<ref>Interview with Roy Thomas and Jerry Bails in ''The Justice League Companion'' (2003) pp. 72 \u2013 73</ref>\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nThe Squadron Sinister are created by the cosmic entity the [[Grandmaster (Marvel Comics)|Grandmaster]] to battle the champions of the time-traveling [[Kang the Conqueror|Kang]] \u2013 the superhero team the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]. The Whizzer battles Avenger [[Hawkeye (Clint Barton)|Goliath]], but the fight is interrupted by the [[Black Knight (Dane Whitman)|Black Knight]]. The Avengers eventually defeat the Squadron and they in turn are abandoned by the Grandmaster.<ref>''The Avengers'' #69\u201371 (Oct. \u2013 Dec. 1969)</ref> The Squadron reappear in the title ''[[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]]'', reunited by the [[extraterrestrial life|alien]] [[Nebulon (comics)|Nebulon]]. The villains receive greater power in exchange for the planet Earth, and create a giant laser cannon in the [[Arctic]] to melt the [[polar ice cap]]s, thereby covering the entirety of the Earth's surface in water. The superhero team the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]] prevent the scheme and defeat the villains (and Nebulon), with [[Namor the Sub-Mariner]] humiliating the Whizzer.<ref>''The Defenders'' #13\u201314 (May \u2013 June 1974)</ref>\n\nAfter this defeat the Whizzer and his teammates are teleported off-world by Nebulon, returning with an energy-draining weapon. The Squadron Sinister plan to threaten the Earth again but are defeated once more by the Defenders and the Avenger [[Henry Pym|Yellowjacket]].<ref>''Giant-Size Defenders'' #4 (1974)</ref> The character has another brief encounter with several members of the Avengers, who seek a way to separate the power prism of [[Doctor Spectrum]] from fellow Avenger the [[Wasp (comics)|Wasp]].<ref>''The Avengers'' Annual #8 (1978)</ref> The Whizzer disassociates himself from the Squadron Sinister and adopts a new costume and alias, '''Speed Demon'''.\n\nWriter [[Bill Mantlo]] and penciller [[Bob Hall Art by [[Walt Simonson]].]]\nAs a result of mutagenic chemicals concocted under the Grandmaster's mental guidance, Speed Demon possesses superhuman speed, stamina, and reflexes. The character can create [[cyclone]]s (by running in circles) and run up walls and across water. Speed Demon's superhumanly fast thought processes and reflexes enable him to perceive his surroundings while moving at high speeds, pick up objects, and execute complex acrobatic maneuvers.\n\nJames Sanders possesses a master's degree in chemistry.\n\n==Other versions==\n===House of M===\nIn the [[House of M]] reality, Jim Sanders, along with [[Abner Jenkins]], works as a biochemist for [[Atlas (Marvel Comics)|Major Josten]] and [[Dum Dum Dugan|General Dugan]].
